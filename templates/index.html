What are Skin Diseases?

Skin diseases are conditions that affect your skin. These diseases may cause rashes, inflammation, itchiness or other skin changes. Some skin conditions may be genetic, while lifestyle factors may cause others. Skin disease treatment may include medications, creams or ointments, or lifestyle changes.

Skin diseases refer to any condition that affects the appearance or function of the skin. There are many different types of skin diseases, each with their own set of symptoms and causes.

Skin Type and Skin Diseases

Actinic Keratosis

It appears as rough, scaly, or crusty patches or bumps on the skin.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

Often appears as a raised, pearly, or waxy bump that may bleed or develop a crust.

Dermatofibroma

Firm, small, reddish-brown bumps that can vary in size from a few millimeters to a centimeter in diameter.

Melanoma

Often appears as a dark, irregularly shaped mole or spot on the skin, but it can also be pink, red, or flesh-colored.

Melanocytic nevi

Typically appear as small, dark, or flesh-colored spots or bumps on the skin, and may be flat or raised.

Vascular lesions

Small, bright red bumps that can appear anywhere on the body, but are most commonly found on the torso.
